$k$-means is used here as a diagnostic rather than as a decorative preliminary.
It answers a question the classifiers cannot: is the normal/abnormal distinction
geometrically present in each feature space \emph{before any label is used}? We
sweep $k \in \{2,\dots,8\}$ on the training split, and measure agreement of the
partition against two references --- the diagnosis and the recording site ---
so that a null result against the diagnosis is still informative.

Table~\ref{tab:clustering} reports the $k=2$ result. The adjusted Rand index
against the diagnosis is \num{0.018} for MFCC, \num{0.028} for log-Mel and
\num{0.002} for PWP: all three are close to chance. Against the recording site
the same partitions score \num{0.201}, \num{0.043} and \num{0.159}. For MFCC and
PWP the site agreement exceeds the class agreement by an order of magnitude. The
same pattern holds at other $k$: for MFCC at $k=3$, ARI reaches \num{0.271}
against the diagnosis but \num{0.420} against the site.

Fig.~\ref{fig:projections} shows the corresponding PCA and t-SNE projections,
in which no domain visibly separates the two classes. We stress that the
site/diagnosis asymmetry is established by the index values above and not by
these plots: the projections are coloured by diagnosis only, and in any case
t-SNE cluster sizes and inter-cluster distances are not quantitatively
meaningful, so a projection is at best corroborating evidence for a partition
statistic. The comparison of ARI against two references is the actual
measurement.

Two observations follow. First, the expectation we started from --- that the
diagnosis would be recoverable without labels --- is contradicted. Second, and
more usefully, the null result is not uninformative, because the site comparison
identifies what the dominant variance actually encodes. The class boundary in
these spaces is a thin supervised direction inside a geometry organised
primarily by how and where the recording was made. This is the first of three
independent indications pointing the same way, and it is the reason we treat the
aggregate metric of Section~\ref{sec:classification} with caution rather than
reporting it as a headline.

The moderate silhouette values (\num{0.396} for MFCC, \num{0.388} for PWP) should
not be read as evidence of good clustering: at $k=2$ the MFCC partition is
extremely unbalanced (\num{29089} versus \num{1606} windows), and a silhouette
computed on one large and one small compact cluster is easy to obtain. Davies--Bouldin
and Calinski--Harabasz degrade sharply for $k \geq 3$ in every domain, which is
consistent with there being no strong natural partition at any $k$ we tested.
